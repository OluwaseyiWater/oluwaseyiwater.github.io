About Me

I am currently a structured master's student at the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences and the University of Cape Town. Prior to this, I was an undergraduate student at the Olabisi Onabanjo University (OOU), where I graduated with a Bachelor's degree in Electrical and Electronics Engineering. I am also serving as a research assistant at the Wireless Communication Lab under the supervision of Dr. Matthew Olajide and Dr. Peter Alao. My final year project involved developing a 100-m wireless transceiver using the fs1000a transmitter-receiver module.

I have a keen interest in information theory and wireless communications, and I am particularly passionate about applying cutting-edge techniques such as reinforcement learning, cryptography, and quantum technologies to tackle the evolving challenges of this domain. Through my research and expertise, I am committed to fostering innovation and enhancing global connectivity.
